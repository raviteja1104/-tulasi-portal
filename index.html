Sacred Conduct
While Plucking Tulasi
  • 1Bathe or wash hands and body before approaching the Tulasi plant
  • 2Remove footwear — never stand with footwear near the Tulasi plant
  • 3Offer a short prayer to Tulasi Mata before plucking — recite the mantra in Section 3 below
  • 4
    Correct Plucking Technique Approach with complete devotion — never use fingernails
    Left Hand

    Hold the stem or branch gently to support and steady the plant while plucking

    Right Hand

    Use fingertips respectfully to detach the leaf with its petiole (small stem) intact

    Tulasi — Ocimum tenuiflorumPluck only fresh, whole leaves with the small stem attached. Do not offer fallen, broken, or wilted leaves to the deity.
  • 5Pluck each leaf with its small stem (petiole) intact — never detach the leaf blade alone
  • 6Do not offer fallen, broken, insect-damaged, or wilted leaves to the deity
  • 7Approach with a calm, devoted mind — never hurriedly or mechanically
Step 3 — Recite Before Plucking

Tulasi Plucking Mantra

✦
Plucking Prayer · Recite before taking leaves from the plant
तुलस्यामृतजन्मासि सदा त्वं केशवप्रिया ।
केशवार्थे चिनोमि त्वां वरदा भव शोभने ॥
Tulasyāmṛta janmāsi sadā tvaṃ keśava priyā |
Keśavārthe cinomi tvāṃ varadā bhava śobhane ||
Meaning — O Tulasi, you are born of nectar and are ever beloved of Lord Keshava (Vishnu). I pluck you only for the purpose of His worship. O auspicious one, be gracious and grant me your blessings.
Important Override

When Any Day Is Permitted

✦
Sacred Exception
For Saligrama & Vishnu Worship —
All Restrictions Are Lifted
Tulasi leaves may be plucked on any restricted day — including Ekadashi, Sunday, or during an eclipse — when required for Saligrama or Vishnu worship. The sanctity of that worship takes absolute precedence.
Leaves may be plucked on the permitted day before a restricted day and stored reverently. This is standard practice before Ekadashi.
Previously plucked or dried Tulasi leaves are fully accepted and auspicious for Vishnu or Saligrama puja on any day, without restriction.
